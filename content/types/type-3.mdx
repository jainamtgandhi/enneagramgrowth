## At a Glance

Type Threes are energetic, success-oriented, and highly adaptable. They excel at reading what others value and becoming that. At their best, Threes are authentic, inspiring leaders. At their worst, they become image-obsessed, deceptive, and emotionally hollow.

## Core Motivation

To feel valuable, to be affirmed, to distinguish themselves through achievement. Threes fear worthlessness — the terrifying possibility that without their accomplishments, they are nothing.

## The Heart Center Connection

As a Heart Center type, Threes wrestle with shame, but unlike Twos and Fours, they manage it by outrunning it — staying busy, productive, and impressive. The cost is disconnection from their own feelings.

## Wings

- **3w2 (The Charmer):** More interpersonal, warm, and people-oriented. Seeks success through relationships and charm.
- **3w4 (The Professional):** More introspective, creative, and serious. May struggle more with authenticity questions.

## Growth & Stress

- **Growth (Arrow to 6):** Threes become more cooperative, loyal, and willing to be vulnerable. They learn to value loyalty over image.
- **Stress (Arrow to 9):** Threes become disengaged, apathetic, and numbed out. They lose their drive and check out emotionally.

## Passion, Virtue & Fixation

Every Enneagram type has a **passion** (the emotional habit that keeps you stuck), a **virtue** (the quality that emerges in health), and a **fixation** (the cognitive pattern that reinforces the passion).

- **Passion:** Vanity (Deceit) — Performing a polished, false self that matches whatever earns admiration, gradually losing touch with authentic feelings beneath the image.
- **Virtue:** Truthfulness (Authenticity) — In health, Threes find the courage to be honest about who they are beneath the resume, letting themselves be seen rather than marketed.
- **Fixation:** Vanity — A preoccupation with self-image and how they appear to others, constantly adjusting their presentation to maintain a winning impression.

## Common Misidentifications

Often confused with Types 7 (both energetic and optimistic) and Type 8 (both assertive and driven). The distinction: Threes are driven by image and admiration; Eights by power and autonomy.

## Reflection Prompts

- Who are you when no one is watching?
- What feelings are you avoiding by staying busy?
- Can you name a time you chose authenticity over approval?
