## At a Glance

Type Eights are powerful, dominating, and self-confident. They approach life head-on, taking charge and protecting those in their circle. At their best, Eights are magnanimous, heroic, and deeply caring. At their worst, they become domineering, ruthless, and destructive.

## Core Motivation

To be self-reliant, to prove their strength, to resist weakness, to be important in their world. Eights fear being harmed or controlled by others — and so they seize control first.

## The Body Center Connection

As a Body Center type, Eights have the most direct relationship with anger among all types. While Ones suppress it and Nines fall asleep to it, Eights express it freely and immediately. Their physicality is central to their experience — they move through the world with force and presence.

## Wings

- **8w7 (The Maverick):** More energetic, entrepreneurial, and restless. Combines power with enthusiasm and appetite.
- **8w9 (The Bear):** More steady, patient, and receptive. More grounded but can be more stubborn and slow to move.

## Growth & Stress

- **Growth (Arrow to 2):** Eights become more caring, open-hearted, and willing to be vulnerable. They learn that strength includes tenderness.
- **Stress (Arrow to 5):** Eights become secretive, detached, and fearful. They withdraw and lose their characteristic confidence.

## Common Misidentifications

Often confused with Type 3 (both driven and assertive) and Type 6 counterphobic (both challenging and confrontational). The distinction: Eights confront from a position of power; counterphobic Sixes confront from a position of anxiety.

## Reflection Prompts

- What does vulnerability feel like in your body? Where do you hold it?
- Who have you pushed away in the name of protecting yourself?
- What would it look like to lead with gentleness instead of force?
