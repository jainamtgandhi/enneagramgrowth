## At a Glance

Type Sevens are busy, fun-loving, and spontaneous. They are the most future-oriented type, always planning their next experience or escape. At their best, Sevens are deeply grateful, present, and joyful. At their worst, they become impulsive, escapist, and unable to sit with difficult emotions.

## Core Motivation

To maintain freedom and happiness, to avoid pain and deprivation, to keep their options open. Sevens fear being trapped in emotional pain — and so they keep moving.

## The Head Center Connection

As a Head Center type, Sevens respond to fear by racing ahead of it — reframing pain as possibility, planning their way out of discomfort. Their mental agility is a defense: if they can stay excited about the future, they don't have to face what hurts in the present.

## Wings

- **7w6 (The Entertainer):** More loyal, anxious, and relationship-oriented. Combines enthusiasm with a need for security.
- **7w8 (The Realist):** More assertive, materialistic, and driven. More grounded but can be more aggressive in pursuit of desires.

## Growth & Stress

- **Growth (Arrow to 5):** Sevens become more focused, contemplative, and comfortable with depth. They learn to stay with one thing instead of chasing the next.
- **Stress (Arrow to 1):** Sevens become critical, perfectionistic, and irritable. Their usual optimism gives way to frustration and rigidity.

## Common Misidentifications

Often confused with Type 3 (both energetic and optimistic) and Type 2 (both warm and upbeat). The distinction: Sevens are driven by avoidance of pain; Threes by pursuit of admiration.

## Reflection Prompts

- What are you running from when you plan your next adventure?
- What would it feel like to stay — fully — in this moment?
- Can joy coexist with sadness?
