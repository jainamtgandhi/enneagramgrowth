## At a Glance

Type Twos are warm, caring, and interpersonal. They orient toward others' needs, often before recognizing their own. At their best, Twos are genuinely generous and emotionally attuned. At their worst, they become possessive, manipulative, and martyred.

## Core Motivation

To be loved, to express their feelings, to be needed and appreciated. Twos fear being unwanted or unworthy of love. They cope by becoming indispensable to others.

## The Heart Center Connection

As a Heart Center type, Twos are deeply attuned to shame — specifically, the shame of not being lovable for who they are (rather than what they do for others). Their identity often becomes wrapped up in their relationships.

## Wings

- **2w1 (The Servant):** More principled, altruistic, and self-critical. May combine helping with a sense of moral duty.
- **2w3 (The Host):** More charming, ambitious, and image-conscious. More outgoing but may give strategically.

## Growth & Stress

- **Growth (Arrow to 4):** Twos become more self-aware, honest about their feelings, and comfortable with their own needs.
- **Stress (Arrow to 8):** Twos become aggressive, controlling, and demanding. They may lash out when their giving isn't reciprocated.

## Common Misidentifications

Often confused with Type 9 (both accommodating and warm) and Type 6 (both loyal and relationship-oriented). The distinction: Twos actively move toward people to earn love; Nines merge to avoid conflict.

## Reflection Prompts

- When you help someone, what are you hoping to receive in return — even unconsciously?
- What would it look like to ask for what you need directly?
- Can you let yourself be loved without earning it first?
