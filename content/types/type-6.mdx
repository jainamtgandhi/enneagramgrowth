## At a Glance

Type Sixes are committed, security-oriented, and often anxious. They can be both the most loyal and the most skeptical of all types — constantly scanning for what could go wrong. At their best, Sixes are courageous, reliable, and deeply committed. At their worst, they become paranoid, defensive, and reactive.

## Core Motivation

To have security, to feel supported by others, to have certainty and reassurance. Sixes fear being without support — being without guidance and unable to survive on their own.

## The Head Center Connection

As a Head Center type, Sixes are the most visibly fear-driven. While Fives retreat and Sevens advance, Sixes oscillate — sometimes compliant (phobic), sometimes counterphobic, challenging the very things they fear. This push-pull is central to understanding Six.

## Wings

- **6w5 (The Defender):** More introverted, analytical, and independent. Relies more on systems and knowledge for security.
- **6w7 (The Buddy):** More outgoing, playful, and socially oriented. Seeks security through connection and humor.

## Growth & Stress

- **Growth (Arrow to 9):** Sixes become more relaxed, trusting, and accepting. They learn to sit with uncertainty without spiraling.
- **Stress (Arrow to 3):** Sixes become competitive, image-conscious, and workaholic. They try to earn safety through achievement.

## Common Misidentifications

Often confused with Type 1 (both dutiful and rule-oriented) and Type 2 (both loyal and relationship-focused). The distinction: Sixes are driven by fear and the search for safety; Ones by internal standards of rightness.

## Reflection Prompts

- What would security actually look like — and is it achievable?
- When you test someone's loyalty, what answer would satisfy you?
- What does courage look like when you're afraid?
