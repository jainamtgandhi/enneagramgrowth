## Three Ways of Knowing

Before diving into individual types, the Enneagram starts with a simpler question: **How do you process the world?**

The nine types are organized into three groups of three, called Centers of Intelligence (or triads). Each center is associated with a core emotion and a way of engaging with reality.

## The Body Center (Types 8, 9, 1)

**Core emotion:** Anger
**Intelligence:** Gut knowing, instinct, physical presence

Body Center types process life through action and instinct. They have a strong, immediate sense of what's right or wrong, fair or unfair. Their relationship with anger defines much of their inner experience:

- **Type 8** expresses anger openly and directly
- **Type 9** falls asleep to anger, numbing it to preserve peace
- **Type 1** suppresses anger, channeling it into standards and self-control

When healthy, Body types are grounded, present, and take honest action from a place of equanimity. When stuck, they become reactive, tense, or numb — losing connection with their heart and head.

Body types often ask: *"Is this just?"*

## The Heart Center (Types 2, 3, 4)

**Core emotion:** Shame
**Intelligence:** Emotional attunement, identity, connection

Heart Center types process life through feelings and relationships. They are highly attuned to how they are perceived and how others feel. Their relationship with shame shapes their sense of self:

- **Type 2** outpaces shame by becoming indispensable to others
- **Type 3** outperforms shame by achieving and winning admiration
- **Type 4** sits in shame, sometimes romanticizing their suffering as depth

When healthy, Heart types relate to others from a stable sense of self, offering compassion and authentic connection. When stuck, they become trapped in image management — people-pleasing, performing, or withdrawing into emotional intensity.

Heart types often ask: *"Am I valued?"*

## The Head Center (Types 5, 6, 7)

**Core emotion:** Fear
**Intelligence:** Analysis, foresight, mental mapping

Head Center types process life through thinking and planning. They scan for threats, seek understanding, and try to create certainty in an uncertain world. Their relationship with fear drives their behavior:

- **Type 5** retreats from fear by withdrawing and accumulating knowledge
- **Type 6** faces fear head-on (or runs from it) in an anxious back-and-forth
- **Type 7** outruns fear by staying positive, busy, and focused on possibilities

When healthy, Head types use mental clarity as a tool for navigating the world — insightful, innovative, and grounded. When stuck, they get trapped in mental loops — overthinking, catastrophizing, or scattering into constant stimulation.

Head types often ask: *"Am I safe?"*

## Why Centers Matter

Understanding your center is often the first step toward understanding your type. Before asking "What number am I?" — ask yourself:

- Do I lead with my gut, my heart, or my head?
- Which emotion do I struggle with most — anger, shame, or fear?
- How do I typically respond when stressed — action, feeling, or thinking?
