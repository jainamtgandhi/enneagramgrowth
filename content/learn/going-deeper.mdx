## Levels of Health

One of the most important — and often overlooked — ideas in the Enneagram is that each type exists on a **spectrum of health**. Your type doesn't change, but the level at which you express it does.

- **Healthy:** You use your type's gifts consciously. A healthy One is wise and fair; a healthy Seven is deeply present and grateful.
- **Average:** Autopilot is running. You're functional but reactive — operating from habit rather than awareness.
- **Unhealthy:** Your type's defense mechanisms dominate. The pattern that was meant to protect you now controls you.

Health levels are dynamic — they shift based on stress, growth work, relationships, and life circumstances. The goal isn't to be "healthy" all the time. It's to notice where you are and gently move toward awareness.

## Practices for Growth

### The Recovery Rhythm
We will inevitably fall back into autopilot. Growth is not about never falling — it's about recovering balance faster. Like a pilot constantly course-correcting, we learn to notice deviations sooner and return to center more gracefully. The gap between "I fell into my pattern" and "I noticed I fell into my pattern" is where transformation lives.

### Daily Check-In
At the end of each day, ask yourself:
- When did my type pattern show up today?
- Was I operating from a healthy or reactive place?
- What would the growth direction of my type have looked like?

### Journaling Prompts
Use the reflection prompts at the end of each type page as journal starters. Write freely — don't edit or judge.

### Body Awareness
The Enneagram is not just a cognitive system. Pay attention to where you feel your type in your body — tension, openness, contraction, warmth.
