## A Powerful Tool Requires Care

The Enneagram can be profoundly helpful. It can also be used in ways that are reductive, harmful, and unfair. The difference lies entirely in how you approach it.

## The Ground Rules

### 1. Share your own type; never assign someone else's.

It's one thing to say "I'm a Six, and I notice my anxiety shows up as over-planning." It's another thing to say "You're such an Eight" to someone who hasn't asked.

Typing others, especially without their consent, turns a tool for self-reflection into a tool for judgment. Don't do it.

### 2. Hold your type loosely.

Your type is a lens, not a life sentence. It describes patterns you tend toward, not a fixed identity. If you catch yourself saying "I can't help it, I'm a Seven", that's the Enneagram being used as an excuse, not a mirror.

### 3. Avoid stereotyping.

- Not all Twos are doormats
- Not all Eights are aggressive
- Not all Fours are dramatic
- Not all Fives are cold

Every type has a full spectrum of health. Reducing people to their worst traits is harmful, even if you're doing it to yourself.

### 4. This is not therapy.

The Enneagram can complement professional support, but it cannot replace it. If you're dealing with trauma, mental health challenges, or deep relational pain, please work with a qualified professional.

### 5. Context matters.

Culture, upbringing, gender, race, neurodivergence, and life experience all shape how a type manifests. Two Type Sixes can look completely different based on their context. Honor the complexity.

## Signs You're Using It Well

- You're more compassionate with yourself and others
- You're noticing patterns, not judging them
- You're growing, not just categorizing
- You're curious, not certain

## Signs You Might Be Misusing It

- You're typing everyone you know
- You're using type as an excuse for behavior
- You're feeling superior to certain types
- You're reducing complex people to a single number

## The Invitation

Use the Enneagram as a mirror for self-awareness. Not a diagnosis. Not a label. Not a weapon. A mirror.
