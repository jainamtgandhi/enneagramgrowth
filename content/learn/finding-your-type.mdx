## No Test Can Tell You Who You Are

Here's the truth most Enneagram websites won't say: **no quiz can reliably tell you your type.** Online tests measure behavior, but the Enneagram is about motivation — the "why" behind the "what."

Two people can behave identically for completely different reasons. A Type 1 and a Type 3 might both be high achievers, but the One is driven by internal standards while the Three is driven by external validation.

## Our Approach

Instead of a scored quiz, we use a guided process called **Discovery**. It works in two phases:

### Phase 1: Find Your Center
The first step is narrowing down your center of intelligence — Body, Heart, or Head. This is often easier to identify than your specific type because:

- Your relationship with anger, shame, or fear is usually recognizable
- The center you "lead with" shows up across all areas of life
- It narrows the field from nine possibilities to three

### Phase 2: Explore Within Your Center
Once you've identified your likely center, you explore the three types within it through reflective questions. These aren't scored — they're designed to help you recognize yourself.

## Tips for Self-Typing

1. **Read with your gut, not your ego.** The type that makes you slightly uncomfortable — not the one that sounds flattering — is often closest.

2. **Think about motivation, not behavior.** Don't ask "What do I do?" Ask "Why do I do it?"

3. **Consider your worst moments.** Your type often shows up most clearly in stress, not in your best moments.

4. **Give it time.** Many people initially mistype. Your type often becomes clearer over weeks or months of reflection.

5. **Read about multiple types.** Most people relate to 2-3 types. Read the full descriptions, not just the headlines.
