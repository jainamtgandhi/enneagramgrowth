## Beyond Your Number

Your Enneagram type is not a box, it's a starting point. Three key concepts make the system dynamic: wings, arrows, and levels of health.

## Wings

Your wing is one of the two types adjacent to your core type on the Enneagram circle. It adds a "flavor" to your type:

- A **Type 1** might lean toward **1w9** (more idealistic, detached) or **1w2** (more warm, action-oriented)
- A **Type 5** might lean toward **5w4** (more creative, emotional) or **5w6** (more practical, collaborative)

You typically favor one wing more, though both may influence you at different times. Your wing doesn't change your core type, it shades it.

## Arrows (Lines of Connection)

Each type is connected to two other types by lines on the Enneagram symbol. These connections describe how you shift under different conditions:

**Growth direction:** When you are healthy and growing, you take on the positive qualities of your growth arrow type. This often feels like expansion, accessing something new.

**Stress direction:** When you are under stress or pressure, you take on the less healthy qualities of your stress arrow type. This often feels like regression, falling into old patterns intensified.

### The Growth & Stress Arrows

| Type | Growth → | Stress → |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1 | 7 | 4 |
| 2 | 4 | 8 |
| 3 | 6 | 9 |
| 4 | 1 | 2 |
| 5 | 8 | 7 |
| 6 | 9 | 3 |
| 7 | 5 | 1 |
| 8 | 2 | 5 |
| 9 | 3 | 6 |

**Important:** These are tendencies, not rules. Growth isn't linear, and stress doesn't make you "become" another type.

## Levels of Health

Within each type, there's a spectrum of health, from deeply unhealthy to profoundly healthy. The same core motivation can manifest as:

- **Healthy:** Wisdom, generosity, courage
- **Average:** Compensation, defensiveness, autopilot
- **Unhealthy:** Destructive patterns, compulsion, disconnection

Growth in the Enneagram isn't about becoming a different type. It's about moving toward the healthier expression of your own type.

## What This Means for You

- You are not one static thing
- Your stress patterns are not failures, they're signals
- Your growth path is specific to your type, not generic self-help advice
- Understanding arrows helps you recognize when you're expanding and when you're contracting
