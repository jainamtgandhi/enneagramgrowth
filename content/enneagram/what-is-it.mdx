## An Ancient Map for Modern Self-Understanding

The Enneagram is a system of nine interconnected personality types that describes core motivations, fears, and patterns of behavior. Unlike most personality frameworks, it focuses on *why* you do what you do, not just *what* you do.

## Origins

The word "Enneagram" comes from the Greek *ennea* (nine) and *gramma* (written or drawn). The symbol, a nine-pointed figure within a circle, has roots in multiple wisdom traditions, though its modern psychological form was developed primarily in the 20th century through the work of Oscar Ichazo and Claudio Naranjo.

## How It Works

Each of the nine types represents a distinct worldview, a set of core beliefs, motivations, and fears that shape how a person thinks, feels, and acts. No type is better or worse than another. Each has its own gifts, blind spots, and path of growth.

The system includes:
- **Three Centers of Intelligence** (Body, Heart, Head)
- **Wings** (adjacent types that influence your core type)
- **Arrows** (lines of growth and stress connecting types)
- **Instinctual variants** (self-preservation, social, one-to-one)
- **Levels of development** (health spectrum within each type)

## What Makes It Different

The Enneagram is dynamic. You are not a fixed label, you move along spectrums of health, draw from your wings, shift along your arrows, and express your type differently depending on context, culture, and maturity.
