## Mistyping Is Normal

Most people misidentify their type at least once. It's a natural part of the process — and it's actually useful, because understanding *why* you mistyped reveals something about how you see yourself.

## Why Mistyping Happens

1. **Behavior vs. motivation.** You identify with a type's behavior without examining the underlying motivation.
2. **Idealization.** You choose the type you *want* to be rather than the one that actually describes your patterns.
3. **Wing confusion.** Your wing is strong enough that you identify with the adjacent type instead of your core.
4. **Cultural conditioning.** Gender norms, cultural expectations, and family dynamics can mask your true type.
5. **Health levels.** A healthy Eight looks very different from an unhealthy Eight — and may resemble another type entirely.

## Common Confusions

### 1 vs. 6
Both are responsible and dutiful. **Ones** follow internal standards of rightness. **Sixes** seek external security and guidance.

### 2 vs. 9
Both are warm and accommodating. **Twos** move toward others to earn love. **Nines** merge with others to avoid conflict.

### 3 vs. 7
Both are energetic and optimistic. **Threes** are driven by image and admiration. **Sevens** are driven by avoiding pain and limitation.

### 4 vs. 5
Both are withdrawn and introspective. **Fours** seek to express their emotional identity. **Fives** seek to understand through detached observation.

### 5 vs. 9
Both can appear calm and disengaged. **Fives** withdraw deliberately to conserve energy. **Nines** withdraw unconsciously to avoid conflict.

### 6 vs. 8 (counterphobic)
Both can be confrontational. **Counterphobic Sixes** challenge from anxiety — they push against fear. **Eights** challenge from a position of power and autonomy.

## How to Get Clearer

- Read full type descriptions, not just headlines
- Focus on what feels uncomfortably true, not flattering
- Ask: "What is my core fear?" rather than "What do I do?"
- Give it time — clarity often comes over weeks, not minutes
- Talk to someone who knows the system well
